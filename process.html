STEP 1

Model Analysis & Objective Definition

We begin by thoroughly understanding your AI system, its intended purpose, and establishing clear, measurable testing goals aligned with your business objectives.

What We Do:
  • Comprehensive Documentation Review: Analyze model architecture, training data sources, and intended use cases
  • Stakeholder Consultation: Collaborate with your team to identify success criteria and potential risk areas
  • Use Case Mapping: Map real-world scenarios where the model will be deployed
  • Testing Scope Definition: Establish specific metrics, benchmarks, and evaluation criteria
  • Risk Assessment: Identify critical failure points, edge cases, and compliance requirements

Why This Step Matters

Without a clear understanding of your AI model and its objectives, testing efforts can miss critical issues or focus on irrelevant metrics. This foundational step ensures our evaluation is:

Comprehensive

Efficient

Optimized resource allocation

Ongoing Monitoring Optional

Continuous quality assurance ensures your AI maintains performance as it evolves, user behaviors change, and new edge cases emerge.

Continuous Testing Includes:
  • Performance Tracking: Monitor model accuracy, reliability, and response quality over time
  • Drift Detection: Identify when model performance degrades or behavior changes unexpectedly
  • Scheduled Re-evaluation: Periodic comprehensive testing to catch emerging issues
  • New Edge Case Testing: Test against newly discovered failure modes and scenarios
  • Version Comparison: Compare performance across model updates and releases
  • Proactive Alerts: Real-time notifications when issues are detected
